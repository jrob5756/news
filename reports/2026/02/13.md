# News Summary for February 13, 2026

## Summary

Today's news reflects a pivotal moment in AI development as major players solidify their strategic positions. Microsoft declares AI self-sufficiency as an explicit goal, signaling intent to compete directly with OpenAI rather than just resell their innovations. Google releases a major upgrade to Gemini 3 Deep Think, achieving breakthrough scientific reasoning benchmarks including passing marks on "Humanity's Last Exam." OpenAI partners with Cerebras to release GPT-5.3-Codex-Spark, marking their first production model on non-Nvidia hardware with 1000+ tokens/second inference speeds. Meanwhile, Anthropic closes a massive $30B funding round at $380B valuation, becoming the second-largest private tech financing round on record. The theme across all developments: inference speed and strategic independence are now as important as raw capability.

## Top 3 Articles

### **1. [Microsoft's AI Chief Targets AI Self-Sufficiency and OpenAI Independence](https://winbuzzer.com/2026/02/13/microsoft-mustafa-suleyman-ai-self-sufficiency-openai-mai-models-xcxwbn/)**

**Source**: WinBuzzer  

**Date**: February 13, 2026

**Detailed Summary**: 

Microsoft has announced a fundamental strategic pivot toward AI self-sufficiency, marking a significant departure from its long-standing dependence on OpenAI. Mustafa Suleyman, Microsoft's AI Chief and former Google DeepMind co-founder, revealed in an interview with the Financial Times that the company is "pursuing true self-sufficiency in artificial intelligence."

Key developments driving this shift:
- **MAI Models**: Microsoft's in-house MAI model lineup includes MAI-1-preview, trained on 15,000 Nvidia H100 GPUs, positioning them to compete with frontier models from OpenAI and Google
- **Infrastructure Investment**: The company has developed the Maia 200, its second-generation AI-accelerator chip designed for cost-efficient inference, and is building the Fairwater network of AI data centers featuring some of the world's largest supercomputers
- **Timeline**: Internal MAI models are expected to be released later this year
- **Partnership Dynamics**: Following the October 2025 corporate restructuring, Microsoft retained a 27% stake in OpenAI (valued at ~$135B) and secured model access through 2032, creating a strategic window for gradual transition

Suleyman made bold predictions about AI's impact, suggesting many white-collar computer-based jobs could be fully automated within 12-18 months. Microsoft is also diversifying AI partnerships, paying Anthropic to host Claude models on Azure and offering Meta's Llama and Mistral models alongside its growing portfolio.

### **2. [Gemini 3 Deep Think: Advancing Science, Research and Engineering](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-3-deep-think/)**

**Source**: Google Blog  

**Date**: February 12, 2026

**Detailed Summary**: 

Google has released a major upgrade to Gemini 3 Deep Think, its specialized "System 2" reasoning mode built for modern challenges in science, research, and engineering. The update was developed in close partnership with scientists and researchers to tackle tough problems where solutions lack clear guardrails and data is often incomplete.

Benchmark achievements:
- **48.4% on Humanity's Last Exam** (without tools) - a benchmark designed to test the limits of frontier models
- **84.6% on ARC-AGI-2** - verified by the ARC Prize Foundation
- **3455 Elo on Codeforces** - competitive programming challenges
- **Gold-medal level performance** on the International Math Olympiad 2025
- **Gold-medal results** on written sections of 2025 International Physics and Chemistry Olympiads
- **50.5% on CMT-Benchmark** - advanced theoretical physics

Real-world applications demonstrated include turning sketches into 3D-printable files, with Deep Think analyzing complex shapes and generating manufacturing files. Early tester Lisa Carbone, a mathematician at Rutgers University working on high-energy physics mathematical structures, used Deep Think to identify a subtle logical flaw in a technical paper that had passed human peer review unnoticed.

Available now to Google AI Ultra subscribers in the Gemini app, with API access opening to select researchers, engineers, and enterprises through an early access program.

### **3. [Introducing GPT-5.3-Codex-Spark Powered by Cerebras](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-3-codex-spark/)**

**Source**: OpenAI  

**Date**: February 12, 2026

**Detailed Summary**: 

OpenAI has released GPT-5.3-Codex-Spark, their first production AI model to run on non-Nvidia hardware, marking a significant milestone in their January partnership with Cerebras. The model delivers code at over 1,000 tokens per second, designed specifically for real-time software development where responsiveness matters as much as intelligence.

Technical highlights:
- **Ultra-low latency**: Runs on Cerebras' Wafer-Scale Engine 3 (WSE-3), a purpose-built AI accelerator
- **Infrastructure improvements**: 80% reduction in client/server roundtrip overhead, 30% per-token overhead reduction, 50% faster time-to-first-token via persistent WebSocket connections
- **Context window**: 128k tokens, text-only at launch
- **Benchmarks**: Strong performance on SWE-Bench Pro and Terminal-Bench 2.0 while completing tasks in a fraction of the time compared to GPT-5.3-Codex

The strategic significance extends beyond raw performance. OpenAI emphasized that "GPUs remain foundational across our training and inference pipelines" but Cerebras "complements that foundation by excelling at workflows that demand extremely low latency." This dual-hardware approach signals a maturing inference infrastructure strategy.

The model is available as a research preview for ChatGPT Pro users in the Codex app, CLI, and VS Code extension, with API access rolling out to select design partners. OpenAI plans to expand to larger models, longer context lengths, and multimodal input as they learn from developer feedback.
